## Patterns Observed

### Content Stack
Every hero follows the same vertical order without exception:

1. Pre-headline badge/pill (event, news, promotion)
2. Headline — always the largest element on the page
3. Sub-headline — 1–3 lines, much smaller, muted weight
4. Primary CTA (+ optional secondary)
5. Social proof logo strip

Nothing breaks this order. No product UI, no testimonials, no feature lists above the logo strip.

### Headline Typography
All five use large, centered headlines with high contrast on their backgrounds. Two dominant approaches:
- **Bold heavy sans** (Dovetail, Superhuman): confident, direct, authoritative
- **Mixed serif italic + roman sans** (Origin, Giga): the italic serif handles the emotionally charged word, sans carries the rest — "Simplify *your* money", "*Own* your wealth", "AI that talks like a human"

The mixed approach is a current trend worth using intentionally. It works when one word carries the weight of the whole headline.

### Pre-Headline Badge
4 of 5 use a small pill or badge above the headline for time-sensitive or newsworthy content: a product launch, funding round, or limited-time offer. These are always:
- Small — never competing with the headline
- Positioned above the headline with clear spacing
- Often include a chevron or arrow if they're clickable

Do not use this element for evergreen marketing copy — it loses credibility fast.

### CTA Treatment
- **Single CTA is strongly preferred.** Giga and Origin (scroll) each use one. When there's a secondary, it's clearly subordinate (outlined vs. filled, or much lower contrast).
- Dovetail uses filled white + outlined side by side — works on dark backgrounds because filled white has natural dominance.
- Superhuman uses a single filled brand-color button with an arrow — the arrow signals action, not just confirmation.
- Never two filled CTAs at the same visual weight.

### Background Approaches
Three approaches used here, all full-bleed:
1. **Atmospheric photography** (Giga, Origin, Superhuman): landscape or sky imagery, always with enough overlay/contrast to keep white text legible. Evokes emotion, fits human-centered or premium products.
2. **Dark abstract pattern** (Dovetail): geometric grid with subtle texture. Tech/enterprise feel. Confident without being cold.
3. **Gradient/color wash** (Superhuman): lavender-to-sky gradient with a person. Bridges photography and design.

Flat white or solid-color heroes are conspicuously absent from current best-in-class SaaS. Full-bleed is the expectation.

### Social Proof Logos
4 of 5 include a customer logo strip at the bottom of the hero. All follow the same rules:
- White or low-opacity monochrome logos on dark, gray logos on light
- Horizontal row, evenly spaced
- No company names below logos (the logo is enough)
- Subtle — social proof, not a feature

Superhuman skips logos and uses floating product UI panels as proof instead — this works when the product UI itself is compelling and recognizable.

### Product Visualization
Two approaches:
- **Floating UI fragments** (Superhuman): UI panels scattered atmospherically around the hero — suggests product richness without being a formal screenshot
- **Device mockup** (Origin): phone emerging from the bottom of the hero — works best when the product is primarily mobile

Neither approach uses a clean, framed product screenshot as the hero visual. The product is suggestive, not demonstrative, at this stage.

## Cross-Cutting Principles

**One message, full commitment.** Each hero has one headline that carries the entire value proposition. Sub-headlines clarify, they don't add a second message.

**Headlines are untouchable.** No background element, no overlay, no product UI should reduce headline legibility. Text contrast is non-negotiable.

**Restraint on CTAs.** The instinct is to add multiple CTAs to capture different audiences. These heroes resist that. One primary action is enough. A weak secondary (outline, low contrast) is acceptable.

**Dark backgrounds dominate.** 4 of 5 use dark or moody backgrounds. This communicates premium, confidence, and focus. It's the current standard for SaaS and AI products targeting professionals.

**Atmospheric = emotional.** The photography choices (mountains at dusk, cloudy sky, gradient sky with a person) are not product-relevant — they're emotional. They make the product feel significant before a single word is read.
